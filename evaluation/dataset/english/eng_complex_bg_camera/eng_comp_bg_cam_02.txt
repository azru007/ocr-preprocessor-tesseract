Birbal confidently replied, "Okay, your majesty. I shall bring one tomorrow."
The next day, Birbal brought a very poor Brahmin. He was in great need of money and his family was starving.
The Emperor questioned him, "Do you know the task? Are you willing to do it?"
The poor man bowed low and said, "Yes, your majesty, I am willing."
That night, the poor man stayed neck deep in that ice-cold water, shivering. A guard was kept near the pond to watch the poor man.
The next day, the poor man came to the Emperor for getting his reward. Akbar asked, "My man! How did you spend the entire night in that freezing pond?"
The man humbly said, "Your majesty, I spent the night by looking at a lamp that was glowing at the top of the castle."
One of the jealous ministers said, "Ah! That explains it, your highness.
The heat from the lamp gave him warmth.
